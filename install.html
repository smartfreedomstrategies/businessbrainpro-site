Welcome

Let's get your Founders’ Brain set up.

The whole setup, on one page. It happens in the Claude desktop app, and we do it together on our call. Two parts: the install (about fifteen minutes) and the brand interview right after (fifteen to twenty more, a conversation, not a form). Read this once before we talk, then keep it to look back on.

You do not have to do any of this alone. We do every step together on the call, live.

And you cannot break anything. Nothing gets deleted, and every real change asks you first. If a step ever looks off, we stop and sort it.

Before our call

Five minutes of prep and the call itself is smooth. Two accounts, one app, then a short list to have within reach.

  • A Notion account

    The free plan runs everything, and free is all you need. Sign up at notion.so with your email or Google, and pick "for personal use" if it asks.

    Ready when: Notion opens and you see your sidebar.

  • Which Notion plan

    Any plan works, including Free. If Notion hands you a free 30-day Business trial when you sign up, take it; just don't enter card details unless you want to keep paying for Business afterward. The fine print is in Quick answers, at the bottom of this page.

  • Claude Pro

    $20 a month, and the free plan will not work for this: plugins and Cowork need a paid plan. Sign up or log in at claude.ai, then Settings, Billing, subscribe to Pro.

    Ready when: your account shows Pro.

  • The Claude desktop app

    Download it at claude.com/download (Mac or Windows, the steps are identical), install it, and log in with the SAME account you just upgraded. The website and the phone app cannot do the setup.

    Ready when: Claude opens as its own app and you are signed in.

  • Your logins, within reach

    The number one thing that stalls a setup is hunting for a password halfway through. Two minutes now saves the headache.

And for the interview, have these handy

Right after the build, your Founders’ Brain interviews you about your business. Fifteen to twenty minutes, conversational, no wrong answers. It goes fastest when you have:

  • A link to your website or your main social profile

    Or any doc that describes your business. Give it that and it drafts most of your profile on its own; you just correct what it got wrong.

  • Your offers, with prices

    Everything you sell, what each thing costs, and which one is the main offer right now.

  • An honest picture of where the business is today

    The system shapes itself around the truth, not the ambition.

That is all the homework there is. You talk, it writes.

The setup, step by step

We will do these together on the call. Here is the whole path so you know what is coming.

  1. Create your Founders’ Brain project, and give it a folder

    In the Claude desktop app, create a new project and name it whatever you like. Before you finish, click Add folder and pick an empty folder on your computer (make a new one called "Founders Brain" in your Documents if you need to). This is your home base.

    The folder is not optional here. It is where your system keeps its own working instructions, so it knows how you like things done instead of starting cold every session. It also ties this project to this computer, which is exactly right for your home base.

    Later you will add two or three tiny projects for your phone, with no folder, thirty seconds each. Apart from those, this is the only project you ever create in Claude. Your launches, offers, and clients get "projects" too, but those live in Notion, and your Founders’ Brain builds them for you.

    You'll know it worked when the project shows up in the sidebar and opens to a fresh, empty chat.

  2. Copy your Notion template

    Go to thefoundersbrain.com/template and click Duplicate (the icon at the top right). That drops a fresh, private copy into your own Notion. This is where your Founders’ Brain keeps everything.

    You'll know it worked when a page called Command Center — Template appears in your own Notion sidebar.

  3. Let Claude see your Notion

    Connect the Notion connector in Claude and click Allow. This is what lets your Founders’ Brain read and build inside your workspace. It only ever touches your own workspace, nothing else.

    You'll know it worked when Notion shows as connected in your Connectors list.

  4. Get your files from me

    I send your files to you directly: the core (command-center.plugin), any add-ons you have (compass.plugin, ghostwriter.plugin, outreach.plugin), and one text file for your phone setup (capture-web-project-instructions.md, used further down). Save them somewhere you can find them (your Downloads folder is fine).

    You'll know it worked when you can see the plugin file (or files) in your Downloads.

  5. Add each plugin in the Claude desktop app

    Same quick path for every file. Do the core first, then each add-on the same way.

    Customize → Personal plugins → + → Create plugin → Upload plugin → pick the file I sent

    You'll know it worked when each one appears in your Personal plugins list.

  6. Restart the app, and check one setting

    Fully quit and reopen the Claude desktop app. Plugins only wake up on a clean start, so this one matters.

    While you are in there, one switch to check. Open Settings, then Cowork, and make sure "Run new tasks in the cloud" is OFF. Your automatic reports need to run on this computer: that is where they can properly reach your Notion and keep your live dashboards working. Run them in the cloud and they can quietly lose access to things they built for you.

    Settings → Cowork → "Run new tasks in the cloud" → off
  7. Say the word

    Open a new chat inside your project and say set up my command center. That is the moment your Founders’ Brain comes alive and starts building your system with you.

    You'll know it worked when it answers by asking for your business name and your first name. That is it waking up.

What happens when you say it

The play-by-play after "set up my command center", so nothing on the call feels surprising.

  1. Two quick questions

    Your business name and your first name. That is all the typing in the whole build.

  2. A taste while it works

    It offers to look at your website (or one sentence about what you do) and reflects your business back at you while the build runs. Say yes; it also makes the interview faster later.

  3. It checks your copy of the template came in clean

    And quietly repairs the small things Notion sometimes bends when a page gets duplicated. You will not see this part; it just happens.

  4. It makes the workspace yours

    Your brand on the title, your seven zones labeled, a few welcome entries so nothing opens looking dead.

  5. It switches on your automatic reports

    The morning brief every day at 7, a who-is-going-cold check on Mondays, and two Friday reports (the patterns in your ideas, and the weekly recap). Your first report lands tomorrow morning.

  6. Then the brand interview, straight away

    Fifteen to twenty minutes of conversation about who you serve, what you sell, and how you sound. This is what the prep list at the top of this page is for.

  7. And the finale

    It builds you one real, branded asset you can use the same day. That is the moment it clicks.

The build itself takes six to eight minutes and it narrates while it works. If it asks you something mid-run, that is normal and good. Answer in plain English. There is no wrong answer.

What arrives on its own, once you're set up

The build switches these on for you. You never ask for them; they just show up. Wondering if they're on? Say are my automatic reports switched on? in any chat.

  • Every morning at 7: the morning brief

    What's due, what's overdue, what moved, plus one of yesterday's ideas picked out and proposed for development. Your first one lands the morning after setup.

  • Monday morning: the who-is-going-cold check

    Leads and clients slipping quiet, with follow-up tasks queued for the most important ones (never more than five, so it never floods you).

  • Friday afternoon: two reports

    The patterns in your ideas (the themes you keep circling, clustered from the last month), then the weekly recap: done, moved, captured, and what's stalling.

  • With add-ons, the list grows

    Outreach adds the 8:00 relationship list (who's waiting on you, message drafted). Compass folds your goal verdict into the morning brief. Ghostwriter can add weekday writing candidates ("candidates every morning") and a Friday content look-back; those two are yours to switch on when you want them.

They run on your computer, switched on once during the build, nothing to maintain. And nothing is lost if your machine was off: a report scheduled for 7 while your laptop was shut simply runs when you next turn the computer on and open Claude. Start at 8:45 and your 7 o'clock brief arrives then. It waits for you rather than passing you by.

Waking up each add-on

Every add-on installs the same way (step 5 above), but each one wakes up with its own first words, said in a new chat inside your project. Here is each one's, and what happens next.

  • Add-on Compass

    Say I have a new goal, or just say the ambition with a number in it ("20 new clients by December"). It interviews you for a few minutes about the goal and the numbers that prove it is moving, then builds the whole tracking structure in Notion: the goal, the metrics, the habit that keeps it moving, and a live dashboard. From then on you just talk: "three sales calls today", "ran 5 miles", "am I on track?". Your goal verdict also joins your morning brief on its own, so every day starts with where you stand.

    You'll know it worked when it starts asking about your goal instead of asking what Compass is.

  • Add-on Ghostwriter

    Say extract my voice. It studies your own writing so everything it drafts sounds like you, not like AI. No published writing yet? Say run the brand foundation instead: a short strategy interview that gives it a base. Either way it then offers a menu of optional brand documents (your beliefs, your visual style, and so on); the voice one is the only must. After that, "write my newsletter" and "what should I write today?" just work. And if you want the day's writing candidates waiting for you every weekday morning, say "candidates every morning" once and they join your automatic reports.

    And no, this is not the brand interview again: that one taught your system your business. This one tunes the writing.

    You'll know it worked when it asks you for samples of your writing.

  • Add-on Outreach

    Say set up outreach. It readies your People tracker for relationship work and switches on the morning relationship list (who is going cold, who you owe a reply, with the message already drafted). From then on: "I met someone at an event", "what do I reply?", "who's going cold?". It writes; you always press send.

    You'll know it worked when it confirms your relationship radar is on.

The core needs no magic words beyond the first one. "Set up my command center" runs once; after that its brand interview, brand brief, and first built asset flow on their own, and every later session starts with "catch me up".

Your phone, part one: Capture (everyone gets this)

One piece lives on claude.ai in the browser instead of the desktop app: Capture, your phone quick-add. It is included with the core, and it is the part you may end up using most. An idea hits you mid-walk, you say it to your phone, one word comes back ("Noted."), and by evening it is filed in the right place in Notion.

  1. Create one small project, with no folder

    In your browser, go to claude.ai, open Projects, and create a new project called Capture. Do not add a folder to this one. A project with a folder only works on the computer that folder lives on, and this one has to work from your phone, so it stays folder-free. That is the whole difference between this and your home base.

  2. Paste in the Capture instructions

    Open the file I sent you named capture-web-project-instructions.md, select all of it, and copy. Then open the new project's settings, find Project instructions, and paste everything in. That file IS Capture; there is nothing to install.

  3. Check Notion is on for that project

    Same Notion connector as step 3 of the install; just make sure it is enabled for this project too.

  4. Test it from your phone

    Open the Claude app on your phone, open the Capture project, and say: capture this: test idea from my phone.

    You'll know it worked when you get one short word back and the idea shows up in your Notion a moment later. In the evening, say tidy up and it files everything from the day.

Have Compass? Add the Daily Log project (five more minutes)

Your on-the-go scorekeeper. You say what happened ("ran 5 miles", "closed the deal", "slept 7 hours") and it logs it on the spot, streaks and all.

  1. Create a second small project, again with no folder

    Same place as Capture: Projects, new project, call it Daily Log. No folder on this one either, for the same reason: it has to work from your phone.

  2. Paste in the Daily Log instructions

    Open the file I sent you named compass-daily-log-web-project-instructions.md, select all of it, copy, and paste everything into this project's Project instructions.

  3. Check Notion is on, then test from your phone

    Open the Daily Log project in the phone app and say: ran 5 miles today.

    You'll know it worked when it logs it immediately and answers with the score moving ("Day 3 in a row"), and the entry shows in your Daily Log tracker in Notion.

Two habits that keep it clean: start a new conversation each day (every day is a fresh page), and keep FACTS here. A new idea to build goes to Capture; this project is for what already happened.

Have Outreach? Add the Outreach project (five more minutes)

For catching a person while they're still warm: you walk out of an event, dictate who you met, and get the follow-up message back on the spot while they land in your People tracker.

  1. Create one more small project, no folder

    Projects, new project, call it Outreach. Same rule: no folder, so it works from your phone.

  2. Paste in the Outreach instructions

    Open the file I sent you named outreach-web-project-instructions.md, select all of it, copy, and paste everything into this project's Project instructions.

  3. Check Notion is on, then test from your phone

    Open the Outreach project in the phone app and say: I met Alex at an event tonight, runs a design studio, wants help with launches.

    You'll know it worked when it hands you a short follow-up message you could send right now, and Alex appears in your People tracker in Notion.

Good to know: your morning relationship list is switched on from the desktop app, not here. This phone project is for the moment itself: quick saves, quick replies, "who's waiting on me today?"

Already set up? Adding an add-on or re-installing

If your Founders’ Brain is already running and you just need to add an add-on (or re-load a plugin after an update), skip everything above. It is three quick steps, no re-doing Notion.

  1. Save the file I sent

    For an add-on it is a new .plugin. For an update it is the newer version of one you already have.

  2. Upload it the same way

    An update just replaces the old one.

    Customize → Personal plugins → + → Create plugin → Upload plugin → pick the file
  3. Restart, then use it

    Quit and reopen the app, start a new chat inside your project, and say the add-on's first words (they're in "Waking up each add-on", just above). No re-duplicating Notion, no re-doing your setup.

Something not working?

  • Not all your tools showed up

    Usually an older file or an app that needs a restart. Ask me for the latest file, re-upload it (step 5), then fully quit and reopen the desktop app. Plugins only reload on a clean start.

  • The file won't upload

    Make sure you are on the latest Claude desktop app and uploading the .plugin file exactly as I sent it (not unzipped, not renamed). If it still refuses, send me a screenshot and I will sort it.

  • It says it can't find your template

    The duplicate did not happen, or it landed under an odd name. Open Notion, copy the link of the template page in your sidebar, paste it into the chat and say "set up my command center". It takes it from there.

  • It's building, but nothing shows up in Notion

    Almost always the permission popups: click Allow when Claude asks to write to Notion (there is an option so it stops asking every time). And double-check Notion is connected with the same account you duplicated the template into.

  • Your morning brief didn't arrive

    First, give it a moment: these run on your computer, so if it was off at 7, the brief runs shortly after you turn the machine on and open Claude, not at 7 sharp. If it still has not appeared with the computer on, then it is the automations themselves: open a chat and say "my morning brief didn't arrive, are my automatic reports switched on?" and it checks them and switches back on anything missing. Also worth re-checking that "Run new tasks in the cloud" is still OFF (Settings, Cowork). Any time you want the picture right now rather than waiting, say catch me up.

  • The app won't open, or it's running slow

    That is before your Founders’ Brain is even involved. Restart the app, and your computer if it is sluggish, update to the latest version, then try step 5 again.

Quick answers

  • Do I have to pay for Notion?

    No. The free plan runs the entire system for one person. The only subscription is Claude Pro.

  • Do I need the paid Notion plan?

    No. Any plan works, and writing to your workspace is never affected. The one difference on Free and Plus: reading your trackers back (your daily briefing, the money tools) can hit an hourly rate limit now and then. Nothing breaks; the report just comes back thinner, and it clears within the hour.

    Signed up fresh with a work email? You likely got a free 30-day Business trial, which runs everything at full strength. One caution: if you enter card details during the trial, it converts to paid Business when the trial ends. No card, no charge; it just drops back to Free.

  • Do I need Notion AI?

    No, skip it. Your Founders’ Brain does the thinking, and it works better than the built-in one. Save the money.

  • Why does it feel slower or flatter some days?

    Usually the chat just got very long. Open a fresh chat inside your project and say "catch me up". You lose nothing: your system's memory lives in Notion, not in the chat.
